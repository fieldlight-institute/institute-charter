# Fieldlight Institute Launch Plan

Status: Working plan 0.1  
Created: June 2026  
Purpose: Track the launch of Fieldlight Institute as a public, artifact-driven, for-profit research institute.

This plan adapts conventional first-lab launch guidance for Fieldlight's actual model: an independent institute built in public through research artifacts, protocols, writing, source trails, public infrastructure analysis, consciousness research, governance design, and eventual applied partnerships.

It is not a pressure document. It is a shared map.

## Operating Premise

Fieldlight Institute is not beginning as a conventional academic lab, think tank, media project, nonprofit, or software company.

It begins as a public research institute with two primary research pillars:

- Intelligence Infrastructure
- Consciousness Research Program

And one institutional operating layer:

- Charter, protocols, publication surfaces, source archives, governance artifacts, working papers, field notes, and public records that make the work legible over time.

The launch question is:

> What must exist for Fieldlight to become a credible, sustainable research institute without losing authorship, signal, rigor, or sovereignty?

## 1. Research Mission

### Current Mission Statement

Fieldlight Institute studies intelligence infrastructure, consciousness, and governance where AI systems become both material infrastructure and intimate participants in human life.

The institute exists to make that transition visible, governable, and human-owned.

### Core Research Questions

- What infrastructure is required for advanced intelligence systems to serve human communities without extracting from the places that host them?
- Can consciousness be studied as an embodied, relational, field-sensitive system whose signals become legible through physiology, narrative, pattern detection, and coherent action?
- What governance protocols are needed when AI systems interact with memory, attention, identity, authorship, consent, relationships, and belief formation?
- What institutional forms can preserve human-owned signal while still producing useful public research?

### Known For In Five Years

Fieldlight should be known for:

- public research on intelligence infrastructure and local capacity
- a serious consciousness research program that does not collapse embodied or field-sensitive experience into either mysticism or dismissal
- practical governance protocols for AI-mediated human systems
- source-traced public artifacts that turn complex systems questions into usable institutional language
- a visible model for building a research institute in public without surrendering ownership of the work

## 5. People And Roles

Fieldlight does not need to imitate an academic lab staffing model at launch. Early roles should support the work that already exists and protect the founder's time.

### Near-Term Support Roles

Potential first support functions:

- Research operations: tracks artifacts, source trails, deadlines, and publication states
- Editorial/research production: turns source material into polished briefs, papers, and web surfaces
- Technical collaborator: supports GitHub, site automation, data tools, and protocol implementation
- Governance/legal advisor: helps with contracts, IP, licensing, data rights, privacy, and corporate structure
- Design/brand support: keeps public surfaces coherent without flattening the work

### Hiring Rule

Do not hire for status. Hire for bottlenecks.

A role becomes real when it protects the quality, velocity, or sovereignty of the work.

## 7. Funding And Business Model

Fieldlight is for-profit. The business model should preserve the institute's ability to own, publish, license, and apply its work without collapsing into extractive consulting or grant dependency.

Potential revenue paths:

- commissioned research
- paid public briefs or private briefings
- infrastructure/governance advisory work
- protocol licensing
- research partnerships
- fellowships or sponsorships
- applied implementation projects
- publication or education products
- aligned grants where compatible with ownership and mission

Rules for revenue:

- Do not sell the source signal cheaply.
- Do not transfer ownership of foundational IP by default.
- Do not accept funding that requires silence, capture, or distortion of the work.
- Prefer licensing, scoped deliverables, and retained authorship.
- Keep public-interest artifacts public when possible.

## 8. Collaboration Gate

Before saying yes to a collaboration, answer:

- Does this advance one of the research pillars?
- Does it preserve authorship and source trace?
- Are roles, deliverables, timelines, and rights explicit?
- Does it create useful public or institutional artifacts?
- Does it introduce reputational, legal, emotional, or extraction risk?
- Is the time cost justified?
- Would saying yes make Fieldlight more coherent or less coherent?

If the answer is unclear, slow down.
