# Fieldlight Institute Charter

Status: Founding draft 0.1  
Created: June 2026

Fieldlight Institute is an independent research initiative for studying intelligence infrastructure, consciousness, and governance at the point where AI systems become intimate participants in human life.

Fieldlight begins from a simple claim: intelligence is no longer only a capability inside machines. It is becoming infrastructure. It is entering homes, bodies, relationships, memory, decision-making, public institutions, and the material systems that keep society running.

The institute exists to make that transition visible, governable, and human-owned.

## Purpose

Fieldlight Institute develops public research, protocols, and institutional artifacts for a world in which intelligence systems touch human beings not only through screens, but through memory, prediction, attention, infrastructure, land, water, energy, authorship, and belief.

The work is organized around two connected premises:

- Intelligence is becoming infrastructure.
- Infrastructure is becoming intimate.

If AI is infrastructure, then its physical substrate matters: data centers, power, cooling, land, minerals, labor, jurisdiction, capital, and local governance.

If AI is intimate, then its human substrate matters: consciousness, memory, identity, consent, authorship, perception, attention, relationships, and the body.

Fieldlight holds those questions together.

## Commitments

### Human-Owned Signal

Human beings generate high-value signal through living: writing, deciding, noticing, remembering, sensing, creating, revising, refusing, and changing over time.

Fieldlight rejects the premise that lived signal is ambient resource material for platforms, models, markets, or institutions to absorb by default.

Lived data is not exhaust. It is the residue of a life in motion.

### Consent As Architecture

Consent cannot remain a policy paragraph attached after extraction has already occurred.

Fieldlight treats consent as an operating condition: explicit, scoped, revocable, traceable, and enforceable through system design.

No consent state, no access.

### Authorship And Trace

Authorship should be preserved at the point of creation.

Fieldlight supports systems that maintain traceable records of origin, context, contribution, revision, permission, and use. Public work may be visible, but visibility is not surrender. Public authorship should strengthen continuity, not dissolve ownership.

### Local-First Memory

Memory requires a governed home.

Fieldlight favors local-first, human-readable, inspectable, and revocable memory systems over platform-controlled profiles that perform intimacy while relocating authority away from the person.

The self is not a platform object.

### Non-Extractive Infrastructure

Fieldlight studies and builds toward infrastructure that can be used without being owned by those who access it.

This includes licensing access instead of transferring ownership, separating storage from interface, preserving exit rights, and designing systems that can connect without requiring capture.

### Power With A Record

Fieldlight rejects hollow oversight, invisible hierarchy, and institutional theater.

Power should be traceable. Roles should be legible. Contributions should retain authorship pathways. Accountability should be designed into the operating structure, not performed after trust has already failed.

## Research Pillars

### Intelligence Infrastructure

The intelligence infrastructure pillar studies the material, economic, political, and civic systems that support AI.

This includes:

- data centers
- energy and grid capacity
- water and cooling systems
- land use and local governance
- telecom and fiber routes
- supply chains
- public-private financing
- rural and regional capacity
- local-first and edge compute systems
- community rights, monitoring, and refusal

The guiding question:

What infrastructure is required for advanced intelligence systems to serve human communities without extracting from the places that host them?

### Consciousness Research Program

The consciousness research pillar studies consciousness where it becomes observable: in the body before language, in the field between people, in the coherence that organizes interaction, in the stories that route attention, and in the signals that precede conscious explanation.

This program does not begin from the assumption that consciousness is sealed inside the individual skull. It begins from the working hypothesis that human consciousness is embodied, relational, permeable, pattern-detecting, and field-sensitive.

The guiding question:

Can consciousness be studied as an embodied, relational, field-sensitive system whose signals become legible through physiology, narrative, pattern detection, and coherent action?

## Institutional Operating Layer

Fieldlight also develops the artifacts required to make this work legible and usable in public:

- charters
- protocols
- field notes
- research frames
- consent and memory models
- governance templates
- infrastructure worksheets
- working papers
- public briefs
- publication-ready essays

The institute is artifact-driven. Questions should produce records. Records should produce tools. Tools should produce better questions.

## Working Method

Fieldlight is being built in public as a research skunkworks: small, focused, experimental, and willing to work at the edges of existing categories.

The operating method is:

- ask the question clearly
- name the source material
- create the artifact
- expose the assumptions
- preserve the trace
- revise in public
- refuse extraction
- keep the work alive

Fieldlight is not trying to become a conventional think tank, software company, lab, nonprofit, or media project.

It is an institute for building the language, protocols, and infrastructure needed when intelligence becomes both material system and intimate force.
